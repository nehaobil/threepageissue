What is OCEAN ACIDIFICATION?

Ocean acidification is caused by the increasing amounts of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere due to human industrialization. Carbon dioxide is dissolving into the ocean water and decreasing its pH, making it more acidic. The acidic water reduces the amount of carbonate ions in the water, which is vital for many marine organisms.

Many marine organisms cannot form shells due to the high acidity of the ocean, including some keystone organisms. Coral reefs are especially affected by ocean acidification, and without coral reefs, huge ecosystems will collapse. Preventing ocean acidification is so important in order to preserve our environment.
